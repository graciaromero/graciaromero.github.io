Hi, I’m Adrian
Gracia Romero

PhD in Plant Phenotyping and Remote Sensing.

I am a researcher in plant phenotyping and precision agriculture at the Institute of Agrifood Research and Technology (IRTA), a research institution ascribed to the Department of Agriculture of the Government of Catalonia.

My research aims to design and implement high-throughput digital phenotyping workflows across 50+ha multi-crop experimental platforms including cereals (wheat, barley, oats, maize, rice), legumes (beans, peas, lentils, soybean) and other crops (buckwheat, rapeseed).

  • Learn More

    • Main research lines

    UAV-based Plant Phenotyping

    Development of UAV applications for extensive field crop monitoring, modeling of crop yield and phenology, and assessment of key traits such as crop cover, plant height, lodging, and canopy temperature
    Reduced reliance on manual agronomic measurements using scalable remote sensing approaches.

    Crop Physiology

    Implemented vegetation index–based stress detection under drought and nutrient constraints.
    Supported breeding and agronomic teams in identifying climate-adapted ideotypes.
